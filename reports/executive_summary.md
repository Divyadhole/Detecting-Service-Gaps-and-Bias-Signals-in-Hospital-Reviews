# Executive Summary — Hospital Review Concern Signal Analysis

**Generated:** March 2026  
**Dataset:** Google Maps hospital reviews — Bengaluru, India

---

## What Was Done

A keyword-lexicon pipeline was applied to hospital reviews to detect four operationally relevant concern categories:

- **Mistreatment** — rude, ignored, dismissive, negligent language
- **Access Barriers** — wheelchair, ramp, accessible, lift references
- **Delays / Wait-time** — waiting, hours, queue, delayed, slow mentions
- **Safety / Hygiene** — dirty, unclean, infection, unsanitary language

Results were aggregated per hospital and cross-tabulated with sentiment labels. Mismatch cases were identified where labels and concern language conflicted.

---

## Key Findings

### 1. Concern Rates Are Unevenly Distributed
A small subset (~20%) of hospitals account for the majority of concern flags. Delay and wait-time concerns are the most prevalent category across facilities.

### 2. Mistreatment Flags Strongly Predict Negative Sentiment
Mistreatment keywords show the strongest association with negative sentiment labels. This category is the most operationally actionable.

### 3. Mismatch Cases Reveal Quality Assurance Opportunities
- **Type A** (positive label + concern language): ~5-10% of positive-labelled reviews contain concern keywords — these warrant human review for label accuracy or mixed experiences.
- **Type B** (negative label, no flags): reviews expressing general dissatisfaction without specific concern keywords — suggests lexicon gaps in cultural or colloquial language.

---

## What NOT to Conclude

> **Patterns are not proof of intent or systemic negligence.**

- Keyword flags are correlational, not causal.
- Online reviewer populations skew toward extreme experiences; the silent majority is not represented.
- A high concern rate reflects *volume of signals*, not proof of incidents.
- No individual reviewer data was used; no PII is exposed.

---

## Recommended Next Steps

1. **Human-in-the-loop**: A clinical quality officer should review flagged reviews before any operational decision is made.
2. **Lexicon expansion**: Use LDA topics to surface language the keyword lexicon misses (colloquialisms, transliterations).
3. **Longitudinal tracking**: Re-run monthly to detect trend changes at individual hospitals.
4. **Audit trail**: All flag decisions are keyword-traceable — maintain a log for governance and accountability.
5. **Stakeholder briefing**: Share this summary as a signal for targeted investigation, not as evidence of wrongdoing.
